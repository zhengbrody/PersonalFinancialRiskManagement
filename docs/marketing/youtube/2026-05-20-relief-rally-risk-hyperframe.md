## Reddit Comment Draft

Today felt like a good example of why a relief rally does not automatically mean portfolio risk is gone.

The headline was positive: S&P 500 up 1.1%, Nasdaq up 1.5%, Russell 2000 up 2.6%. But the more important driver was that oil pulled back and the 10-year Treasury yield fell from about 4.67% to 4.57%.

From a portfolio risk perspective, that raises a useful question: did the risk actually go away, or did the pressure factor just ease for one day?

If a portfolio was hit hard when yields rose and bounced when yields fell, that portfolio is rate-sensitive. If tech and AI positions all move together, that may be one crowded macro/theme exposure rather than true diversification.

For retail investors, I think today is a good day to ask:

- What happens if yields move back toward 4.7%?
- How concentrated is the portfolio in AI / semis / high-multiple tech?
- If Nasdaq drops 5%, what is the account-level drawdown?
- Is margin involved?
- Did today’s rally actually improve the safety buffer, or just recover one bad day?

Not financial advice. Just a portfolio risk framework.
